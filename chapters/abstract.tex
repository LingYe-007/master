推荐系统已从单纯的流量分发转向对用户深度兴趣的挖掘。异构信息网络（Heterogeneous Information Network）能够整合多种异构数据源、挖掘隐藏的语义信息，是提升推荐精度的可行途径。同时，在《个人信息保护法》与 GDPR 的合规约束下，加之各平台间“数据围墙”加高，传统的集中式大图训练模式难以同时满足跨域融合与隐私合规。联邦学习（Federated Learning, FL）以“数据不动模型动”的方式协同训练，参与方在本地保留原始数据、仅交换经保护的模型参数，有利于满足合规要求。然而在联邦场景下，现有异构图推荐研究仍面临冷启动、推荐性能受限、通信瓶颈与工程落地难等问题。

围绕上述问题，本文从跨视图语义融合与模型压缩两方面入手，提出属性-结构双视图对比学习机制应对用户冷启动，并将语义感知压缩策略用于异构图模型，在冷启动、Non-IID 适配及端侧部署效率上构建了可落地的联邦异构图推荐框架，并给出从算法到系统的完整实现。在 MovieLens-1M、Yelp 和 ACM 上与 FedNCF、FedGNN、FedPerGNN、FedHGNN、FedProto 等五种基线对比，以 HR@K 与 NDCG@K（命中率 Hit Rate at K、归一化折损累积增益 Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain at K）为评估指标，所提模型在 HR@10、NDCG@10 上均优于基线。主要工作包括：
\begin{cnenumerate}
    \cnitem 针对冷启动与数据异质性，提出基于属性-结构双视图对比学习的联邦异构图推荐框架 FedASCL（Federated Attribute-Structure Contrastive Learning）：在潜在空间中通过跨视图对比学习对齐用户属性与图结构语义，以属性特征补偿交互缺失；并引入全局语义原型对齐（Global Semantic Prototype Alignment）约束本地表征、缓解客户端模型漂移（Client Drift）。在冷启动与高异构（Non-IID）设定下，FedASCL 均优于所对比基线。
    \cnitem 为降低通信开销，提出语义感知的模型参数压缩策略，设计轻量级元路径选择器在本地训练中动态剔除低贡献冗余通道，并采用残差梯度量化压缩传输。约 20 倍压缩比下保持推荐精度的同时将通信开销降低约一个数量级（详见第四章）。
    \cnitem 为支撑工程落地，构建了联邦论文推荐系统，采用端云协同分层架构完成模型分发、本地训练、压缩上传与安全聚合；工程实现上业务层可采用异步任务调度（如训练任务队列），联邦轮次本身为同步聚合，与第三、四章实验设定一致。系统集成本地差分隐私模块，完成从算法到系统的闭环验证。
\end{cnenumerate}

\noindent With the rapid development of the mobile internet, recommendation systems have shifted from mere traffic distribution to deep interest mining of users. Heterogeneous information networks (HINs) have emerged as a practical technology to improve recommendation accuracy by integrating diverse heterogeneous data sources and mining hidden semantic information. Meanwhile, under the compliance requirements of the Personal Information Protection Law and GDPR, as well as the increasing ``data silos'' between major platforms, the traditional centralized large-graph training paradigm can no longer meet the demands of cross-domain data fusion and privacy compliance. Federated learning (FL), through a distributed training paradigm of ``models move while data stays'', allows participants to retain raw data locally and only exchange privacy-protected model updates (e.g., parameters or gradients under secure aggregation), thus satisfying privacy compliance requirements. However, due to the characteristics of the federated scenario, existing research on federated heterogeneous graph recommendation still faces the cold start problem, recommendation performance constrained by non-independent and identically distributed (Non-IID) data, and communication bottlenecks and deployment difficulties brought by high-dimensional heterogeneous models.

\noindent This paper addresses the above issues as follows. Starting from cross-view semantic fusion and structured model compression, it proposes an attribute--structure dual-view contrastive learning mechanism for user cold start in federated recommendation and applies a semantics-aware compression strategy to heterogeneous graph models. Cold start, Non-IID adaptation, and efficient edge-side deployment are studied jointly, leading to a privacy-preserving federated heterogeneous graph recommendation framework with both algorithm- and system-level solutions. Experiments on three public datasets (MovieLens-1M, Yelp, and ACM) compare against five baselines (FedNCF, FedGNN, FedPerGNN, FedHGNN, and FedProto) using HR@K and NDCG@K, and the proposed approach achieves favorable results.

\noindent Specifically, the research work of this paper is as follows:

\noindent (1)To tackle the poor recommendation performance caused by cold start and Non-IID data, this paper proposes a federated heterogeneous graph recommendation framework based on federated attribute--structure contrastive learning (FedASCL). The framework employs a cross-view contrastive learning mechanism to align user attribute and graph structure semantics in the latent space and reconstructs missing topological information using attribute features; it also introduces a global semantic prototype alignment mechanism to correct client drift. Experiments show that FedASCL performs favorably in cold start scenarios and under highly heterogeneous data in the settings of Chapter~3.

\noindent (2)To address the problems of large parameter size and low communication efficiency in federated heterogeneous graph models, this paper proposes a semantics-aware model parameter compression strategy. This strategy designs a lightweight meta-path selector to dynamically eliminate redundant channels with low contribution to users during local training, and combines residual gradient quantization technology to compress transmitted data. Experimental results show that at approximately 20$\times$ compression ratio, the strategy maintains recommendation accuracy while reducing communication overhead by approximately one order of magnitude (see Chapter~4), alleviating the communication bottleneck in federated recommendation systems.

\noindent (3)To solve the difficulty in deploying current federated recommendation systems, this paper constructs a federated paper recommendation prototype with an edge-cloud layered architecture (data, algorithm engine, services, and presentation). Federated training follows synchronous rounds with secure aggregation as in Chapters~3--4; asynchronous job scheduling may be used at the service layer (e.g., task queues). A local differential privacy module is integrated to complete the algorithm-to-system loop.
